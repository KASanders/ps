Digital Edition of the Spiritual Meadow (in progress)

John Moschus (fl. 6-7th c. CE) traveled around the Mediterranean interviewing monks, nuns, desert fathers and mothers, scholars, mimes, merchants, soldiers, and prostitutes. By turns riotous and harrowing, the stories collected in his Spiritual Meadow often bear a taste of the absurd, while opening an invaluable window into the lived experience of diverse peoples in Late Anqituity.

As today the text of the Meadow is largely inaccesible, this site aims to produce a digital edition. Currently, only a small sample of stories are available (roughly 5%) and the text (of Migne's Patrologia Graeca) contains many dubious readings. Therefore, besides the completion of encoding, further goals, which are currently the subject of collaboration between students and faculty at The University of the South in Sewanee, TN, include the collation of several manuscripts and the production of an app crit. Interactive indices and integration with mapping platforms will add further layers of visualization

The Greek text was created with Optical Character Recognition technology. It is encoded according to TEI/Epidoc standards and uses the CapiTains framework, which implements the CTS citation scheme that was originally designed for the Homer Multitext Project. Integration with the Alpheios project allows users to parse any word in Greek or Latin by double clicking on the word.
