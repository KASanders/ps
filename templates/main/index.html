Digital Edition of the Spiritual Meadow (in progress)

a project by Kyle Sanders

John Moschus (fl. 6-7th c. CE) traveled around the Mediterranean interviewing monks, nuns, desert fathers and mothers, scholars, mimes, merchants, soldiers, and prostitutes. By turns hilarious and harrowing, the stories collected in his Spiritual Meadow often betray a taste for the absurd, while opening an invaluable window into the lived experience of diverse peoples in Late Anqituity.

As the text of the Meadow remains largely inaccesible and unknown, this site aims to produce a digital edition. Currently, only a sampling of stories are available (roughly 30%), and the source text (Migne's PG) contains many dubious readings. Further goals include the collation of several manuscripts and the construction of an app crit. Interactive indices and integration with mapping platforms will add additional layers of visualization.

The Greek text was created with Optical Character Recognition technology. It is encoded according to TEI/Epidoc standards and uses tools designed by CapiTainS in order to implement the CTS citation scheme. Integration with the Alpheios project means that any Greek or Latin word highlighted by readers will be defined and parsed in browser via a pop-up.
